1) Overview

MicroRacers POL is designed to be simple, transparent, and fully on-chain: the game smart contract stores the state of each car (races used, rewards earned, and retirement status), and determines whether a car is allowed to race.

What you do

Mint a car NFT, select it in your garage, and run races on-chain.

What the contract does

Records results, updates car stats, enforces limits, and marks cars as retired.

Core idea: Each car has a limited number of races and a capped reward potential. Once a car reaches its limits, it becomes permanently retired from racing.

2) Core Concepts

NFT Cars (ERC-721)

Each car is an ERC-721 NFT. Ownership is used for garage access and to determine who can race a given car. The game contract reads and updates each car’s game-related data.

On-Chain State

  • Races Used: How many races the car has already run.
  • Max Races: The maximum number of races the car is allowed to run.
  • Total Rewards: Total rewards earned by the car.
  • Status: Active or Retired.

Player Experience

The UI displays your garage, lets you select a car, and shows real-time information such as races remaining, total rewards, and the last race transaction.

3) Car Lifecycle

Each car follows a predictable lifecycle controlled by the smart contract.

Active

The car can race as long as it has races remaining and has not reached its reward cap.

Retired

The car can no longer race once it reaches max races or the capped reward potential.

Retirement is permanent: Once retired, a car cannot be reactivated. This rule is enforced on-chain.

4) Races & Results

Running a race is an on-chain transaction. The contract determines the outcome and updates the car state. The UI can interpret results and display a clear visual outcome (e.g., 1st / 2nd / 3rd place).

Result Types

  • 1st Place: Highest reward tier.
  • 2nd Place: Medium reward tier.
  • 3rd Place: Lowest reward tier.

State Updates

  • Increase Races Used by 1.
  • Increase Total Rewards based on the result tier.
  • Check limits and set Status to Retired if needed.

5) Rewards & Limits

Rewards are distributed according to the result tier. Each car has a capped reward potential which acts as a hard ceiling. This creates a game loop where cars eventually retire, encouraging progression and churn.

Reward cap: A car cannot exceed its capped reward potential. When the cap is reached, the car retires.

Why caps exist

  • To keep the game loop finite per car.
  • To protect sustainability by limiting rewards per NFT.
  • To create strategic decisions: when to race, when to mint again, and how to manage your garage.

6) Fairness & Randomness

MicroRacers POL uses an on-chain mechanism to determine outcomes. The exact implementation depends on the deployed contract (e.g., pseudo-randomness based on available on-chain data, or an oracle-based system).

Important: Randomness on public blockchains has trade-offs. The project prioritizes simplicity and transparency. Future upgrades may improve randomness robustness.

7) Security Model

Smart contracts enforce the core rules: ownership checks, race eligibility, reward caps, and retirement. The UI is only a convenience layer; it cannot override on-chain rules.

Principles

  • On-chain enforcement: eligibility and retirement are not controlled by the UI.
  • Ownership verification: only the NFT owner can race that car.
  • Deterministic limits: max races and reward cap are contract-enforced.

Security note: Users should always verify they are interacting with the official contract addresses and website.

8) Economy & Sustainability

The game economy is designed around finite reward potential per car and a constant flow of participation. Sustainability depends on the relationship between minting, reward distribution, and participation over time.

Design goals

  • Finite rewards per car via caps and max races.
  • Simple, understandable mechanics suitable for onboarding.
  • Expandable design for future content and features.
Not financial advice: MicroRacers POL is a game. Rewards are gameplay outcomes and may change over time via future versions.

10) FAQ

Do cars reset if transferred?

Car state is stored on-chain. Transferring the NFT transfers ownership, but the car’s races used, rewards, and retirement status remain unchanged.

Can a retired car race again?

No. Retirement is permanent and enforced by the smart contract.

Does the UI control the rules?

No. The UI only reads and displays on-chain state and helps you send transactions.

Will the game be updated?

The project may release new contract versions or new collections. Upgrades may require new deployments.
